Getting Started

  1. Install PaperClip Receiver on your Mac from the Mac App Store — it's free
  2. Launch it — you'll see a paperclip icon in your menu bar. That means it's ready
  3. Open PaperClip on your iPad
  4. Write or draw with Apple Pencil
  5. Tap Send — your drawing arrives on your Mac clipboard instantly
  6. Paste (⌘V) into any app: ChatGPT, Claude Code, Gemini, email, documents

Troubleshooting Connection Issues

"No Mac found" on iPad

If the iPad app says "No Mac found", try these steps in order:

  1. Is PaperClip Receiver running on your Mac? Look for the paperclip icon in your menu bar (top-right of your screen). If it's not there, open the app
  2. Allow Local Network access on iPad. Go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Local Network and make sure PaperClip is enabled. If you denied the prompt when it first appeared, you need to enable it here
  3. Check Wi-Fi on both devices. Both your iPad and Mac need Wi-Fi turned on. They don't need to be on the same network — peer-to-peer works directly — but Wi-Fi must be enabled
  4. Check your Mac firewall. Go to System Settings → Network → Firewall. If the firewall is on, make sure it's not blocking incoming connections for PaperClip Receiver
  5. Restart both apps. Quit PaperClip Receiver on Mac (click the menu bar icon → Quit), relaunch it, then close and reopen PaperClip on iPad

Drawing sent but nothing on clipboard

Check the PaperClip Receiver menu — it shows a count of received images. If the count went up, the image is on your clipboard. Try pressing ⌘V in a different app. Some apps (like Terminal) don't accept image pastes.

Connection drops frequently

Peer-to-peer Wi-Fi works best when your devices are within about 30 feet of each other. The connection will automatically re-establish when the devices are in range again.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the transfer work?

PaperClip sends drawings directly to your Mac over peer-to-peer Wi-Fi using Apple Wireless Direct Link (AWDL). PaperClip Receiver runs in your Mac's menu bar, receives the drawing, and places it on your clipboard. No data passes through any external server.

Do both devices need to be on the same Wi-Fi network?

No. The connection uses Apple's peer-to-peer technology (AWDL), which works directly between devices. Both devices just need Wi-Fi turned on — they'll find each other automatically, even on restricted networks like eduroam.

Where are my notes stored?

Notes are saved locally on your iPad in the app's private storage. They are not uploaded to any server.

Can I use my finger to draw?

Drawing is Apple Pencil only. Use your finger to scroll and navigate the sidebar.
