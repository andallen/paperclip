DESCRIPTION
PaperClip turns your iPad into a handwriting input for AI. Write with Apple Pencil, tap Send, and your handwriting arrives on your Mac — ready to paste into ChatGPT, Claude Code, Gemini, or any other app that accepts images.

Think on paper. Send to AI.

WHY HANDWRITE?

Some ideas are easier to draw than type: equations, diagrams, quick sketches, arrows connecting concepts, etc. PaperClip lets you think with your hands and send the result straight to AI.

HOW IT WORKS

1. Write or draw with Apple Pencil
2. Tap Send
3. Your handwriting is sent directly to your Mac over peer-to-peer Wi-Fi
4. Paste into any AI chat, terminal, or editor

FEATURES

- Full-screen canvas with complete Apple Pencil toolbar
- One-tap send directly to your Mac — no iCloud or clipboard relay needed
- Send modes: crop a region, capture the screen, or grab the full canvas
- Multiple notes with sidebar, search, and rename

PRIVACY

Completely free with all features included. No accounts. No analytics. No data collection. Notes stay on your iPad. Data only leaves to your Mac when you tap Send.

APP REVIEW NOTES (private, only seen by Apple's review team)
PaperClip is an iPad app for handwriting with Apple Pencil. Drawings can be sent to a Mac over peer-to-peer Wi-Fi using Bonjour discovery and Apple Wireless Direct Link (AWDL). The Mac side runs a small companion receiver (PaperClipReceiver) that listens for incoming images and places them on the Mac clipboard for pasting. The iPad app is fully functional standalone for note-taking; the send feature uses standard Apple networking APIs (Network.framework / NWBrowser / NWListener with peer-to-peer enabled). No data is sent to any external server.
