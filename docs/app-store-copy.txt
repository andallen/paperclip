--- START ---
PaperClip turns your iPad into a handwriting input for AI. Write with Apple Pencil, tap Send, and your handwriting arrives on your Mac — ready to paste into ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any other app that accepts images.

Think on paper. Send to AI.

WHY HANDWRITE?

Some ideas are easier to draw than type: equations, diagrams, quick sketches, arrows connecting concepts. PaperClip lets you think with your hands and send the result straight to AI.

HOW IT WORKS

1. Write or draw with Apple Pencil
2. Tap Send
3. Your handwriting is sent directly to your Mac over peer-to-peer Wi-Fi
4. Paste into any AI chat, terminal, or editor

Requires the free PaperClip Receiver companion app on your Mac (available on the Mac App Store). Both devices connect automatically — no configuration needed.

FEATURES

• Full-screen canvas with complete Apple Pencil toolbar
• One-tap send directly to your Mac — no iCloud or clipboard relay needed
• Send modes: crop a region, capture the screen, or grab the full canvas
• Multiple notes with sidebar, search, and rename

PRIVACY

Completely free with all features included. No accounts. No analytics. No data collection. Notes stay on your iPad. Images only travel to your Mac when you tap Send, over your local network.

APP REVIEW NOTES (private, only seen by Apple's review team)
PaperClip is an iPad app for handwriting with Apple Pencil. Drawings can be sent to a Mac over peer-to-peer Wi-Fi using Bonjour discovery and Apple Wireless Direct Link (AWDL). The Mac side uses a companion menu bar app called PaperClip Receiver (bundle ID: me.andy.allen.PaperClipMac), which is a separate Mac App Store submission. PaperClip Receiver listens for incoming images and places them on the Mac clipboard for pasting. The iPad app is fully functional standalone for note-taking; the send feature uses standard Apple networking APIs (Network.framework / NWBrowser / NWListener with peer-to-peer enabled). No data is sent to any external server. To test the send feature, both the iPad app and the Mac companion app must be running on the same network or near each other with Wi-Fi and Bluetooth enabled.

APP REVIEW NOTES (private, only seen by Apple's review team)
PaperClip Receiver is a macOS menu bar companion app for PaperClip on iPad (bundle ID: me.andy.allen.PaperClip), which is a separate App Store submission. It uses Network.framework (NWListener) with Bonjour and peer-to-peer (AWDL) to receive PNG images from the iPad app over the local network. Received images are written to NSPasteboard.general for the user to paste. The app has no main window — it runs as a menu bar extra (LSUIElement = true) with a paperclip icon. The dropdown shows connection status, a received image count, a "Start at Login" toggle (SMAppService), and a Quit button. The app uses standard Apple networking APIs and does not communicate with any external server. To test, run this app on a Mac and PaperClip on an iPad on the same network, then send a drawing from the iPad.
